# Swedish TMA & APV Regulations (Version 5.0)

Updated to reflect the **September 1, 2024** regulatory overhaul (APV v5.0).

## APV (Arbete på väg) Competence Levels (TDOK 2018:0371 v.2.0)

The system is now role-based and recorded in the **ID06** database:
- **Steg 1.1 - 1.3**: Basic competence for all personnel on road work sites.
- **Steg 2.1 (Särskild fordonsförare)**: **Mandatory for TMA drivers**, escort vehicle drivers, and those performing intermittent work.
- **Steg 2.2**: Drivers of maintenance vehicles (plowing, salting, etc.).
- **Steg 2.3 (Skyddsanordningsansvarig)**: Responsible for placing, moving, and removing safety devices (including TMA). A key role for site safety.
- **Steg 3.0**: Site managers and higher responsibility.

## TMA Vehicle Technical Requirements (TDOK 2012:86 v.5.0)

- **Mounting**: Attenuators must be mounted strictly according to manufacturer specs with verifiable documentation.
- **Visibility**: New requirement for **Battenburg patterns** (high-visibility checkering) on the vehicle.
- **Brake Control**: Specific requirements for braking systems to ensure the vehicle remains stationary upon impact.
- **Backing Safety**: Technical warning systems are required when reversing to protect ground personnel.
- **Buffer Zone**: Clear separation zones bakom (behind) the TMA must be maintained (refer to Principal Sketches).

## Operational Categories

- **Fixed Work (Fast arbete)**: Longer duration, static position.
- **Moving Work (Rörligt arbete)**: Constant movement along the road.
- **Intermittent Work (Intermittent arbete)**: Short duration (max 60 mins), high frequency of movement.

## Key Regulatory Documents

- **TDOK 2012:86 (v.5.0)**: Technical requirements for TMA and maintenance vehicles.
- **TDOK 2024:0043**: Principal sketches (Principskisser) for traffic arrangement.
- **TDOK 2018:0371 (v.2.0)**: Competence requirements for road work.
- **TRVFS 2012:7**: Trafikverket's general regulations.

## Protection Classes (Skyddsklasser)

- Determined by road speed and traffic volume (ÅDT).
- TMA is typically required on roads with speed limits >60 km/h.
