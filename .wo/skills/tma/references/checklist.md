# TMA Operational Safety Checklist

## Pre-Departure (Vehicle Check)

- [ ] **Attenuator Status**: Visual inspection for cracks, leaks, or damage. Ensure it locks in both transport and deployed positions.
- [ ] **Carrier Weight**: Verify the vehicle weight is within the approved range for the attenuator model.
- [ ] **Lighting**: Test all warning lights, X2/X3 signs, and arrow boards.
- [ ] **Battenburg Pattern**: Ensure markings are clean and visible.
- [ ] **Reverse Warning**: Confirm the technical reversing alarm/warning system is functional.
- [ ] **Brakes**: Verify the parking/emergency brake system meets the stationary requirement.
- [ ] **Documentation**: Mounting certificate and inspection reports present in the vehicle.

## On-Site Deployment

- [ ] **TA-plan**: Verify deployment matches the approved Traffic Arrangement Plan.
- [ ] **Positioning**: Align the vehicle according to the relevant Principal Sketch (TDOK 2024:0043).
- [ ] **Protection Zone (Skyddsavstånd)**: Ensure the required distance between the TMA and the work area is maintained.
- [ ] **Communication**: Establish clear radio/voice contact with the work team.
- [ ] **Monitoring**: Driver must be alert and monitor traffic approaching from the rear.

## Driver Requirements

- [ ] **ID06**: Personal ID06 card is present.
- [ ] **Competence**: Driver has valid **APV Steg 2.1** certification (less than 4 years old).
- [ ] **PPE**: Personal protective equipment (High-visibility Class 3) is worn.

## Incident Management

- [ ] **Near Miss Reporting**: Log any "tillbud" (near misses) where the TMA was almost hit or traffic behaved dangerously.
- [ ] **Collision**: In case of impact, follow emergency procedures, secure the site, and report to the site manager immediately.
